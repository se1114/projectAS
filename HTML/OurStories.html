Our Stories

Amy's Story

Hiya ladies, I'm Amy! I'm currently on my 4th year of my 6-year medical program at Kings College London. I've personally found university life AMAZING! The cultural enrichment that I've encountered is eye-opening. Despite my busy schedule between hospital rounds, running Hindu society at Kings and competing at dance competitions across the UK, I try to keep myself sane. I'm a strong believer in meditation and prayer and how it helped me cope the past 4 years of uni. I often keep a little book of gratitude where I note down all the things I'm grateful for, and helps make me reflect and stay positive. When I need a break, I often sit down with friends or watch my favorite TV show (you guessed, Grey's Anatomy!). My advice for female students is: Emotional control is key. Be strong about how you feel and stand up for yourself. You are AWESOME and capable of breaking the glass ceiling.

Clio's Story

Hi my name is Clio, and I study English at the University of Bristol! I find university is characterised by intense highs and intense lows, where deadlines often make me overwhelmed. To stay positive I exercise regularly, often I do workouts from Youtube in the comfort of my own bedroom! I also try to leave the house, for no particular reason other than to be outside and I love to cook wholesome meals! My advice for other female students is to find other women that you bond with over specific problems, especially other people from ethnic minorities. Remember men are too privileged often to understand your problems or they’re trying to offset their own with yours. My other advice is to drink tea, wear an outfit that makes you feel great and exercise as often as possible even if you feel lethargic. I used to meditate but I don’t anymore however the principle of calming your mind of external thoughts sticks with me and helps in times of stress

Hannah's Story

I'm Hannah studying Aeronautical Engineering at Cambridge University. How do I find university life? Well, it's been good. I like socialising with people from different courses, countries and race. It allows me to take advantage of the multicultural environment that we have here at Cambridge. Even though, it sometimes comes at the expense of my education considering the extensive workload, I always make sure I maintain a balance. I cope with stress by making time to hang out with my friends, chill out and just have some downtime. I recommend you to join societies where you can meet other females with similar interests, especially if you are studying a very male dominated course. I know, it gets overwhelming being at a renowned university and competing with the smartest people in the world but we all need that thing in our schedule that we enjoy doing and destresses us. I personally don’t exercise, meditate or play sports but I am the Vice President of the Palestinian Society at Cambridge and we've currently been organising events to raise money for the refugees. Contributing to a greater cause, makes me feel good about myself. I suggest you find your 'greater cause'!

Melissa's Story

Hey, my name is Melissa. I'm studying Civil Engineering at University College London. I have found university challenging and only gets harder from one year to the next. The pressure also builds up; pressure to pass, pressure to find a job and pressure from personal situations outside of uni. When I'm stressed I tend to spend time with people I love, I find it therapeutic. I also find it helpful to have someone to lean on, a shoulder to cry on when things get tough. In truth, it's okay to feel down and it's okay to seek help from those around you and adore you. Carrying the burdens and expectations of this age is heavy and having people you trust to speak to will definitely help you get through. Make sure you enjoy what you study, make friends with people on your course so you can help each other and start working on submissions as soon as you get them. I'm not athletic and the university workload means you have to prioritise (going to the gym isnt mine), but I do find going on long walks with my sisters refreshing and destressing.

Penny's Story

Hi my name is Penny and I am currently in my 3rd year of Veterinary Medicine at the Royal Veterinary College in London. My University experience has been incredible, however there have been some bumps along the road. I failed my first year and found out I was dyspraxic, so that was a big learning curve for me! To stay positive at university I love to meet up with friends, and often go to drum n bass nights to dance the night away. On top of that I really love playing in my weekly netball league, and going to the gym. University is stressful, I’m not sure I have fully handled yet, but I think the key is stay organised and plan every day, but not to think too far ahead! My advice for other female students would be to join a sports club or society and try to make an impact within that club, or find a new hobby. Taking time out to focus on something you are passionate about other than your degree can be very fulfilling. I don’t meditate currently, but it’s something I’ve always wanted to get into. I often like to visit my parents, and take my dog for a walk, if I feel very stressed and university life is overwhelmingly busy.

Samantha's Story

Hi! I'm Samantha... studying graduate medicine at Duke NUS, Singapore. I graduated with a BEng in Biomedical engineering from Imperial College London before I moved to Singapore. Imperial … was hard work but graduate medicine ain't no walk in the park either. Yet, medicine is my passion so, I have no regrets and it's onlu up from here. The medicine program at Duke NUS is demanding, intensive and gratifying. I've learnt a lot in the 2 years and a half that I've been at NUS and only 2 more to go! University overall is stressful but not impossible to manage with the right mindset and attitude. I gym, run and love to dance a LOT. I also like to go out for dinner or drinks with friends on Fridays. Not to forget the occasional skype session with my long-distance friend Chloe from London, occasionally motivating and inspiring each other to always aim higher. Chloe and I both know how tough it is to be a female sometimes because of societal expectations. You are supposed to be this and you are supposed to be that. Just be who you want to be and tell society where to get off!!
